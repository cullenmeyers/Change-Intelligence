PayPal total missing shipping in WooCommerce

Reviewed July 13, 2026Evidence confidence: Active support topic; root cause not confirmedPrimary domain: Revenue / shipping reconciliation
Current decisionIf PayPal captured or displayed an order total that does not include shipping, compare PayPal against WooCommerce at the order level before fulfillment and accounting. Do not assume the WooCommerce order total, PayPal total, customer receipt, and payout will all agree.

The WooCommerce PayPal Payments support forum currently lists a topic titled “Shipping not included in Paypal total”. This is exactly the kind of operational payment mismatch that can look small in checkout but create real revenue, support, and accounting consequences.

Shipping can be the hidden mismatch.An order can look paid while the collected PayPal amount, shipping charge, shipping tax, and WooCommerce order total disagree.

What changed?

  • Observed pattern: a current support topic reports shipping not included in the PayPal total.
  • Risk area: shipping lines, shipping tax, coupons, PayPal order creation, and WooCommerce order-total reconciliation.
  • Current status: this is a reported support topic, not a confirmed universal plugin-wide bug.

Who is affected?

This record matters most for stores using WooCommerce PayPal Payments where shipped orders, shipping tax, local delivery fees, coupons, or conditional shipping rules are part of checkout. Stores selling only digital products or orders without shipping lines are less likely to be affected by this specific pattern.

What should you do?

Safest audit sequence

  1. Find the first mismatch. Record plugin versions, WooCommerce version, checkout type, shipping method, taxes, and coupons active at the time.
  2. Compare totals order by order. Check WooCommerce total, PayPal total, shipping line, shipping tax, discount lines, refunds, and payout.
  3. Recreate the cart in staging. Use the same product, address, shipping method, coupon, tax setting, and PayPal funding source.
  4. Test payment creation and capture. Confirm the amount sent to PayPal includes shipping before the customer approves payment.
  5. Pause risky combinations if repeated. Disable only the affected shipping/payment combination when possible instead of taking the whole store offline.
  6. Escalate with evidence. Share system status, order IDs, PayPal transaction IDs, and reproduction steps privately with support.

What is not yet proven?

The public evidence does not prove the affected version range, the exact cause, or whether the mismatch happens before PayPal approval, after capture, or only in a display/export layer. The correct first action is financial reconciliation and reproduction, not assuming all PayPal orders are wrong.

Escalate immediately when: PayPal collected less than WooCommerce recorded, multiple shipped orders are affected, refunds no longer match the collected total, or accounting exports include shipping revenue that PayPal did not collect.
